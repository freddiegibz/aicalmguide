Example

What this looks like in a real business situation.

Act as an assistant for my local service business.

Business type: [plumber / electrician / cleaner / landscaper / etc.]
Main service: [insert service]
Ideal customer: [insert customer type]
Tone: [friendly / professional / direct]

Write a reply to a new customer enquiry that:
1. Thanks them for getting in touch
2. Confirms I can help
3. Asks for the key details I need to quote properly
4. Sounds clear, helpful, and professional
5. Stays short enough to send as a text or email

Then write:
- a simple quote follow-up message for 2 days later
- a polite chase-up message if they have gone quiet
- a short version I can send from my phone

What that turns into

From a real enquiry to a sendable reply in minutes.

Incoming enquiry

"Hi, I need a quote for a garden tidy-up and hedge cut next week. Do you cover Bromley and what availability do you have?"

Reply draft

Hi, thanks for getting in touch. Yes, I cover Bromley and I'd be happy to quote for that. If you send over the garden size, a couple of photos, and whether all waste needs removing, I can give you a clearer quote and let you know the next available slot.

Then the prompt pack helps you write the quote follow-up and chase message too.
